About Compositions™

Your organization is investing in employer brand, internal communications, and change communications.
The question is whether the structural conditions are arranged to make that investment believed.

The Practice

Compositions™ exists because organizations are investing more in communications than the structural conditions beneath it can support.

Every day, HR directors invest in employer brand, communications leaders build internal campaigns, change managers craft announcements, and nonprofit executives write grant narratives — with genuine intent and genuine craft.

But the pattern is consistent: sophisticated audiences don't evaluate communications. They measure it against operating evidence they already hold. They know what gets rewarded. They've seen what happens when commitments meet budget pressure. They've noticed the distance between what leadership says and what the operating decisions confirm.

When that distance is small, communications produces belief. When it is large, no investment in communications quality compensates.

Compositions™ analyzes that distance before it becomes damage — and designs the structural and governance conditions that make the gap self-closing rather than self-widening.

The Work

Compositions™ is not a communications agency. Not a culture consultancy. Not an HR advisory firm.

It is a structural advisory practice: analyzing the conditions that determine whether organizational communications is believed, and designing the governance infrastructure that makes coherence between structural conditions and communications self-sustaining.

The work is structural analysis, architectural, and governance-focused. The deliverables are written structural reads and governance blueprints — designed for implementation by the organization's existing team, not to create dependency on continued outside support.

The Foundation

The lens is built from seven disciplines most advisors treat as separate domains.

Each discipline sees a different dimension of the same structural problem. Together, they produce an analytical capacity no single discipline can match — and that no other practitioner in the employer brand, internal communications, and change communications space currently holds.

Strategic Communications at Enterprise Scale

Reads the gap between what the organization says, what the operating architecture can support, and what stakeholders actually experience. Operates across governance, strategic, execution, and capability transfer layers simultaneously.

Brand Strategy

Reads whether the brand promise — including the employer brand — is structurally real, or performing a reality the organization cannot currently sustain.

Organizational Development at Enterprise Scale

Reads the conditions shaping behavior at scale: decision rights, accountability, capability, role clarity, trust, and adaptation. Grounded in director-level roles at MMGY Global (550 people, 15 offices, 6 countries) and Blue Cross Blue Shield of Kansas City (1,800 employees).

Culture Integration at Enterprise Scale

Reads how operating decisions, incentives, leadership behavior, and norms either reinforce or quietly defeat organizational commitments — and what that means for whether communications about culture will be believed.

Impact & Outcomes Measurement

Reads whether communications investments are producing defensible outcomes — connecting investment, evidence, and stakeholder value in ways that make structural arguments credible to funders, boards, and senior leadership.

Social Epidemiology & Structural Determinant Thinking

Grounds the work in the principle that structural conditions, not intentions alone, shape outcomes across populations. DrPH Candidate, Social Epidemiology, Capella University.

Instructional Design & Permanent Capability Transfer

Ensures the work transfers into permanent internal capability — so organizations don't require ongoing outside interpretation to sustain the governance over time. Master of Liberal Arts in Instructional Design.

Dana Middleton | Structural Advisor

Dana Middleton is a Structural Advisor and the principal of Compositions™, a Kansas City-based advisory practice working at the intersection of organizational communications and the structural conditions that determine whether it's believed.

With 25 years of experience across strategic communications, brand strategy, and culture integration at enterprise scale — including leading the corporate social impact function at MMGY Global and directing change communications and health equity integration at Blue Cross Blue Shield of Kansas City — Dana works with mid-market organizations and nonprofits on a problem the field rarely names directly: the distance between what an organization communicates and what its operating structure can credibly support.

Most communications investment assumes the problem is reach, clarity, or craft. This work starts one layer beneath that — identifying where the structural conditions are producing the gap, and designing the governance that closes it.

She is a doctoral candidate in Social Epidemiology at Capella University and holds a Master's in Instructional Design. Her work is grounded in the conviction that structure, not messaging, determines whether organizational commitments hold.
