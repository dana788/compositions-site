The Work

Before investing in better communications, understand what the structural conditions are already producing.

Three half-day working sessions for organizations whose employer brand, internal communications, change communications, or grant narratives are not producing the return they were designed to produce.

Not another messaging strategy. Not a brand refresh. A structural read of the conditions determining whether the communications is believed — and a governance design that makes credibility self-sustaining.

When to Engage

Engage when the communications is working and the outcomes still aren't there.

—

The employer brand is polished and the right candidates still aren't converting or staying.

—

The internal communications is reaching everyone and employees still aren't moving.

—

The change initiative followed the playbook and adoption isn't there at 90 days.

—

The grant narrative is strong and funders are still asking harder questions than they used to.

—

Leadership communications is consistent and leadership credibility is still declining.

—

The messaging has been refined repeatedly and the same skepticism keeps appearing.

—

Something structural feels off and no communications investment is closing the gap.

These are structural signals.

The Premise

The issue is rarely the quality of the communications.

It is the distance between what the organization is communicating and what its operating conditions can credibly support.

Organizations invest heavily in employer brand, internal communications, change communications, and organizational storytelling.

But those investments often run ahead of the structural conditions that would make them credible — leading to employer brands that current employees don't recognize as true, internal communications that produces reach without belief, and change announcements that produce compliance without adoption.

Employees are not passive recipients of organizational communications. They are continuous observers of organizational behavior. They measure what they are told against what they observe daily — and when the structural conditions and the communications are separated, no investment in message quality closes the gap.

The most effective organizational communications is not the most polished. It is the most accurate.

Closing the gap between what the organization is communicating and what funders, staff, and partners actually verify.

Grant narratives that don't land aren't struggling because they're poorly written. 02

The EVP Clarity Lab™

An employer value proposition built from what is structurally real — not from what leadership hopes is true.

Most EVPs are built from aspiration rather than structural reality — describing the employee experience leadership hopes to deliver rather than the experience current employees would confirm as true. An EVP that current employees don't recognize as describing their experience is not a talent acquisition asset. It is a liability.

This session works through what is genuinely distinctive about working at this organization — the specific structural conditions, not the aspirational ones — and builds the employer value proposition from what the operating conditions can actually confirm.

The EVP is built in the session. The organization leaves with it.

What's Delivered

A drafted EVP, language for job postings and career site copy, and a one-page internal message guide so recruiting teams, hiring managers, and senior leadership carry the same story into every talent conversation. Within 48 hours.

Best For

HR Directors, CHROs, and VP People at companies with 50–500 employees who are actively hiring, experiencing turnover they didn't expect, or whose EVP current employees don't recognize as describing their experience.

03

The Change Communications Layout™

Change communications that produces adoption — not compliance that looks like adoption at 30 days and reveals itself at 90.

Change announcements don't produce the wrong outcomes because the communications was poorly crafted. They produce the wrong outcomes because employees are applying their accumulated evidence about how this organization has honored previous commitments — and what that evidence shows doesn't support the conviction the announcement is performing.

This session analyzes the structural credibility reserve going into the change, identifies what in the current change communications is producing skepticism, and designs the manager enablement approach that converts announcement into adoption.

Manager enablement is where adoption is won or lost. The all-hands reaches everyone. Adoption happens in the conversations between managers and their direct reports. This session equips those conversations.

What's Delivered

A written structural read of the current change communications, specific narrative adjustments most likely to move compliance toward adoption, and a manager communications guide. Within 48 hours.

Best For

CHROs, COOs, and CEOs at organizations navigating restructuring, leadership transition, strategy pivot, rebrand, or new operating model — whose change communications needs to produce adoption, not surface compliance.
