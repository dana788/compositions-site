The Work

Investments in culture, change, brand, and people commitments are not producing the adoption, credibility, or return leaders expected.

The issue is rarely the quality of the initiative or the clarity of the message. It is the structural gap between what the organization is funding, what it is communicating, and what its operating conditions can actually sustain.

Compositions™ analyzes that gap — and designs the governance conditions that make coherence self-sustaining. The entry point is the communications problem. The work is structural. The outcome is credibility that compounds.

When to Engage

Engage when the investment is real, the communications is working, and the outcomes still aren't there.

—

The employer brand is polished and the right candidates still aren't converting or staying.

—

The internal communications is reaching everyone and employees still aren't moving.

—

The change initiative followed the playbook and adoption isn't there at 90 days.

—

The grant narrative is strong and funders are still asking harder questions than they used to.

—

Leadership communications is consistent and leadership credibility is still declining.

—

The messaging has been refined repeatedly and the same skepticism keeps appearing.

—

Something structural feels off and no communications investment is closing the gap.

These are structural signals — not communications problems.

The Premise

The issue is rarely the quality of the initiative or the clarity of the message.

It is the structural gap between what the organization is funding, what it is communicating, and what its operating conditions can actually sustain.

Organizations invest meaningfully in employer brand, internal communications, change communications, people commitments, and grant narratives — with genuine intent and genuine craft.

But those investments frequently run ahead of the structural conditions that would make them credible: leading to employer brands employees don't recognize as describing their experience, internal communications that produces reach without belief, and change announcements that produce compliance without adoption.

Employees are not passive recipients of organizational communications. They are continuous observers of organizational behavior. They measure what they are told against what they see rewarded, what happens when commitments meet operating pressure, and what the last ten promotion decisions actually said about what the organization values.

When the structural conditions and the communications are aligned, the communications builds credibility. When they are separated, no investment in message quality closes the gap.

The most credible organizational communications is not the most polished. It is the most structurally accurate.

EVP Reality Read™  ·  For Mid-Market Organizations

An employer value proposition built from what the structural conditions can currently confirm — not from what leadership hopes is true.

Most EVPs are built from aspiration rather than structural reality — describing the employee experience leadership intends to deliver rather than the experience current employees would recognize as true. An EVP that current employees don't recognize as describing their experience is not a talent acquisition asset. It is a liability.

This session analyzes what is genuinely distinctive about working at this organization — the specific structural conditions, not the aspirational ones — and produces the employer value proposition from what the operating conditions can actually confirm.

The EVP produced in this session is structurally real. That is what makes it hold.

A drafted EVP, language for job postings and career site copy, and a one-page internal message guide so recruiting teams, hiring managers, and senior leadership carry the same story into every talent conversation. Within 48 hours.

HR Directors, CHROs, and VP People at companies with 50–500 employees who are actively hiring, experiencing turnover they didn't expect, or whose EVP current employees don't recognize as describing their actual experience.

Change Credibility Read™  ·  For Organizations Mid-Transition

Change communications that produces adoption — not compliance that looks like adoption at 30 days and reveals itself at 90.

Change announcements don't produce the wrong outcomes because the communications was poorly crafted. They produce the wrong outcomes because employees are applying their accumulated evidence about how this organization has honored previous commitments under pressure — and what that evidence shows doesn't support the conviction the announcement is performing.

This session analyzes the structural credibility reserve going into the change, identifies where the current change communications is producing skepticism, and designs the manager enablement approach that converts announcement into adoption.

Manager enablement is where adoption is won or lost. The all-hands reaches everyone. Adoption happens in the conversations between managers and their direct reports in the weeks that follow. This session equips those conversations.

What's Delivered

A written structural read of the current change communications, specific narrative adjustments most likely to move compliance toward adoption, and a manager communications guide. Within 48 hours.

Best For

CHROs, COOs, and CEOs at organizations navigating restructuring, leadership transition, strategy pivot, rebrand, or new operating model — whose change communications needs to produce adoption, not surface compliance.
